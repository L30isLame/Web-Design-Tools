The Life of a Revolutionary

Steve Jobs was born in San Francisco in 1955, he later co-founded Apple Computer, Inc. in 1976.

In 1985, he was forced out of the company, but returned as CEO in 1996.

During his time as CEO, he was instrumental in the development and launch of various successful products such as the Macintosh, iMac, iTunes, iPhone and iPad.

In 2011, he resigned as CEO of Apple due to health issues and passed away on October 5 that year due to complications from pancreatic cancer.

Major Accomplishments in Steve Jobs Career

  • Highlighted the significance of design in personal technology
  • Developed the Macintosh computer
  • Launched the iMac, iTunes, iPhone, and iPad
  • Created the Pixar Animation Studio

Steve Jobs co-founded Apple and was a key figure in the development of the personal computer industry, revolutionizing the way people interact with technology.
